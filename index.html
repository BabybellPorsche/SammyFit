Day A: Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

Push-ups

Start in a plank position, lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor, then push back up. Keep your body straight throughout the movement.

Dumbbell Bench Press

Lie on a bench, hold dumbbells at chest level, then press them upward until your arms are fully extended. Lower back down with control.

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Sit or stand with dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward. Press the weights upward until your arms are fully extended, then lower back down.

Dips

Support yourself on parallel bars with straight arms. Lower your body by bending your elbows, then push back up to the starting position.

Lateral Raises

Stand with dumbbells at your sides. Raise your arms out to the sides until they're parallel with the ground, then lower back down with control.

Day B: Pull (Back, Biceps)

Assisted Pull-ups

Use an assisted pull-up machine to help you perform pull-ups. Grasp the handles, and pull your body up until your chin clears the bar. Lower yourself back down with control.

Dumbbell Bent-Over Rows

Hold a dumbbell in each hand, bend your knees slightly, and hinge at the hips to lower your torso until it's almost parallel to the floor. Keeping your back straight, pull the dumbbells to your lower ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position with control.

Hanging Leg Raises

Hang from a pull-up bar with straight legs. Raise your legs until they're parallel to the floor, then lower them back down with control.

Face Pulls

Attach a rope to a high pulley and grasp the handles with an overhand grip. Pull the rope towards your face, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower the rope back to the starting position with control.

Dumbbell Bicep Curls

Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward. Keeping your elbows close to your sides, curl the weights up towards your shoulders. Lower the weights back down with control.

Hammer Curls

Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward. Keeping your elbows close to your sides, curl the weights up towards your shoulders, rotating your palms so that they face inward at the top of the movement. Lower the weights back down with control.

Day C: Core Focus

Plank

Get into a forearm plank position with elbows directly under shoulders and legs extended behind you. Maintain a straight line from head to heels by engaging your core and avoiding sagging or piking your hips. Hold this position for the desired duration, focusing on steady breathing.

Leg Raises

Lie flat on your back with your legs extended. Raise your legs until they're perpendicular to the floor, then lower them back down with control.

Ab Roller

Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Hold an ab roller above your chest. Roll the roller out, engaging your abs, then roll it back in with control.

Dead Bug

Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Extend your arms straight above you. Simultaneously raise your arms and legs off the floor, forming a diagonal line. Lower your arms and legs back down with control.
